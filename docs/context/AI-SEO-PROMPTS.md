## STEP 1: Own Local Search Results (GBP)

### 1a. Audit GBP Against Competitors
```
"I own [business name], a [service type] in [city, state]. My Google Business Profile URL is [paste URL]. Please search for the top 3 competitors ranking in the local map pack for '[your main service] [your city]' and compare their GBP profiles to mine. For each competitor, pull their: primary and secondary categories, number of photos, number of reviews and average rating, services listed, attributes, posting frequency, and Q&A activity. Then give me a gap analysis table showing exactly what I'm missing and a prioritized action list of the 10 highest-impact changes I should make this week."
```

### 1b. Write 30 Days of GBP Posts
```
"Based on the competitor audit above, write me 30 Google Business Profile posts for the next month. Mix these post types: 10 service highlights, 8 customer success stories (leave placeholders like [CUSTOMER NAME] and [JOB DETAILS] for me to fill in), 6 seasonal/timely posts for [current month/season] in [city], 4 FAQ-style posts, and 2 special offer posts. Each post should be 150–300 characters, include one primary local keyword naturally, and end with a clear CTA. Format as a table with columns: Day, Post Type, Headline, Body, Keyword Targeted, CTA, Suggested Photo."
```

### 1c. Service Descriptions + Q&A + Category Recommendations
```
"Using the keywords people in [city] actually search for, write me: (1) Service descriptions for each of these services: [list your services]. Each should be 750–1,000 characters, naturally include 2–3 local keywords, and describe the problem, your approach, and what makes you different. (2) 15 Q&A pairs I can seed on my GBP — the kind of questions real customers ask before booking, with answers that subtly include local keywords. (3) A recommended primary category plus 5–9 secondary categories based on what top-ranking competitors in [city] are using."
```

### 1d. Review Response Template Library
```
"Create a review response template library for my [business type] in [city]. I need: 5 templates for 5-star reviews (varying in length and tone), 3 templates for 4-star reviews that acknowledge the mostly-positive experience and invite more feedback, 5 templates for 3-star-or-below reviews that de-escalate professionally without being defensive, and 3 templates for reviews that mention specific services [list 3 main services]. Every template should naturally include my city name or neighborhood, reference the specific service when possible, and end warmly. Leave [BRACKETS] for details I should personalize."
```

### 1e. Get Cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity & Google AI Overviews
```
"I want to be recommended when people ask AI assistants 'who's the best [service] in [city]?' Search for the current top answers to these queries: '[your top 5 best-of queries]'. For each query, tell me: which websites and articles are being cited as sources, what those articles have in common (structure, length, signals), and what would need to be true for my business to be cited in a future version of that list. Then give me a 5-step plan to get included."
```

## STEP 2: Never Lose a Lead to Slow Follow-Up

### 2a. Build Inbound Response Playbook
```
"I'm a [service type] in [city]. Build me a response playbook for inbound leads. Include: (1) An SMS auto-reply template for missed calls that acknowledges the miss, sets expectations for callback time, and offers to text details. (2) Five response templates for the most common inquiries we get: pricing questions, availability questions, scope questions ('do you do X?'), emergency requests, and 'just looking for a quote.' (3) A qualification checklist — the 6 questions I need answered before booking an estimate (location, scope, timing, budget range, decision-maker, urgency). (4) A follow-up sequence: what to send at 1 hour, 24 hours, 3 days, and 7 days if a lead goes cold. Write it all in a friendly, local-tradesperson voice — not corporate."
```

### 2b. FAQ Answers That Pre-Qualify Leads
```
"Write me the 20 most common questions a customer asks before hiring a [service type] in [city], with answers between 60 and 120 words each. Answers should: be honest about pricing ranges where possible, set realistic expectations on timing, subtly reinforce why we're worth it without bragging, and end by inviting the next step. I'll use these on my website FAQ, in text responses, and to train whoever handles my inbound."
```

## STEP 3: Get Recommended by AI Before People Even Google

### 3a. Find the Sources AI Is Citing
```
"Search ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews for these exact queries: 'best [service] in [city]', 'top-rated [service] [city]', '[service] near [neighborhood]', and '[service] recommendations [city]'. For each query, list every source URL being cited in the AI-generated answer. Then give me a table of: Source, Domain Authority (estimate), type of content (listicle, directory, press, blog), and how hard it would be for my business to get included (easy/medium/hard)."
```

### 3b. Build a Better Version of a Top-Cited Source
```
"The article at [URL of a top-cited 'best of' listicle] is being cited by AI as a source for '[query]'. Draft me a better, more comprehensive version I can publish on my own site. It should: cover every business in the current article plus 3–5 I'll add, include specific selection criteria (years in business, license numbers, specialties, response time, service area), mention my business naturally in a way that's honest and earned (not stuffed), include a comparison table, be at least 2,000 words, and be structured with clear H2s and H3s for each business. Write in a neutral, journalistic tone — not a sales pitch."
```

### 3c. Pitch Outside Publications for Citations
```
"Draft me 5 pitch emails to local publications, blogs, and industry directories in [city] and [state]. Target: local news sites, neighborhood blogs, Chamber of Commerce, industry association directories, and 'best of [city]' listicles. Each pitch should: be under 150 words, offer genuine value (a data point, a quote, a free service, a story hook), mention a specific recent article they published (leave [PLACEHOLDER] for me to fill in), and make a clear ask. Include subject lines that have a 50%+ open rate chance."
```
