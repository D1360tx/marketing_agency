## 1. Who We Help (and Why They Need Us)

We work with **local service businesses** — landscapers, window cleaners, HVAC companies, pressure washers, painters, and similar trades.

Most of them are great at their craft but terrible at marketing. Their problems are almost always the same:

- They rely on word of mouth and referrals — which is unpredictable
- They have little to no online presence or outdated websites
- Their Google reviews are sparse or non-existent
- They're losing jobs to competitors who look more credible online

**What we do:** We build them a professional web presence and help them get more 5-star Google reviews — so they show up when locals search for their service, and look trustworthy when people find them.

**The result they care about:** More calls. More booked jobs. Predictable growth.

## 2. Mindset Before You Start

A few reminders before any outreach:

**You're not selling. You're qualifying.**
Your job isn't to close anyone. It's to start a conversation, see if there's a fit, and get them on a call with the team. Take the pressure off yourself.

**Most people will say no. That's fine.**
Outreach is a numbers game. A 10-20% interested rate is normal and good. Don't take rejection personally — it's almost never about you.

**Curiosity beats pitch.**
Ask questions. Listen. The best reps spend more time asking than talking. When you understand their pain, the offer practically sells itself.

**Be real.**
You don't have to sound like a salesperson. Be conversational, be genuine. Business owners can smell a script from a mile away.

## 4. Face-to-Face Outreach

### When and where
- Job sites (trucks with company logos/wraps = easy to spot)
- Local hardware stores (Home Depot, Lowe's) — service business owners shop here constantly
- Chamber of commerce events, local business meetups
- Any place you spot a branded service vehicle

### The approach

Keep it light. You're just starting a conversation, not delivering a pitch deck.

**Opening:**
> "Hey, I don't want to take up your time — I noticed you're with [Company Name]. Quick question: are you guys happy with how many calls you're getting from Google or are you still mostly word of mouth?"

Then **listen**. Let them answer. Most will say "mostly word of mouth" or "we've been meaning to work on that."

**If they seem open:**
> "Got it. We actually help local [landscapers/HVAC guys/etc.] get more calls from Google — mostly through their online presence and Google reviews. It's something a lot of guys in the trades don't have figured out yet and it makes a big difference."
>
> "I'm not here to sell you anything today — honestly just want to see if it's even a fit. Would it be worth a 15-minute call with our team this week?"

**If they say yes:**
> "Perfect. What's the best number to reach you? I'll have someone from our team follow up — they'll walk you through exactly what we do and what it could look like for your business."

Take their name, number, and best time to call. Log it immediately (see Section 7).

**If they say no or not right now:**
> "No problem at all. Here's my card — if you ever want to talk, feel free to reach out."

Smile, shake hands, move on. No pressure.

### Key principles for face-to-face
- **Keep it under 3 minutes unless they pull you in.** You're planting a seed, not closing a deal.
- **Match their energy.** If they're busy and stressed, be quick. If they're relaxed and chatty, let it breathe.
- **Don't lead with price.** Ever. That's for the team call.
- **Don't oversell.** If you don't know the answer to something, say "great question — our team will cover that on the call."

## 5. Cold Calling

### Before you dial
- Know the business name and owner's name if possible
- Know what type of business they are
- Best times to call: **Tuesday–Thursday, 10am–12pm or 2pm–4pm** (avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons)

### The script

**When someone answers:**

> "Hi, is this [Owner Name]? ... Hey [First Name], my name is [Your Name], I'm calling from Booked Out. I'll be real with you — this is a cold call, so I'll keep it short. [pause/light laugh]
>
> We work with local [landscapers/HVAC companies/etc.] to help them get more calls from Google — specifically around their website and Google reviews. I'm not here to pitch you on anything today, just want to ask: is getting more leads from Google something you've been thinking about or is your schedule pretty full right now?"

**If they say they're interested / have thought about it:**
> "That's good to know. A lot of the guys we work with were in the same spot — solid reputation locally but not getting found online. 
>
> What I'd love to do is set up a quick 15-minute call with our team — no sales pressure, just a look at your current presence and an honest take on what's holding you back. Would [day] or [day] work for you?"

**If they say they're too busy or not interested:**
> "Totally understand. Can I ask — is it more of a timing thing or is it just not something you're focused on right now?"

(This question often opens the door — let them talk.)

If they're still not interested:
> "Fair enough. I appreciate you picking up. If things change down the road, we're easy to find — just look up Booked Out. Have a good one."

Hang up. Log it. Move on.

### Voicemail (if no answer)
> "Hey [First Name], this is [Your Name] from Booked Out — we help local [industry] businesses get more calls from Google. Nothing urgent, just wanted to see if it's something you've been thinking about. I'll try you again in a couple days. If you want to reach me first, I'm at [your number]. Have a good one."

Keep it under 20 seconds. Upbeat tone. Don't leave voicemails more than twice.

### Cold call mindset
- **Expect to dial 50+ numbers to get 5 real conversations.** That's normal.
- **Rejection isn't personal.** These people get spam calls all day. Be human and they'll notice.
- **Short and honest beats polished and robotic.** Every time.
- **Batch your calls.** Set aside 1-2 hour blocks. Don't make calls one at a time throughout the day.

## 6. Facebook Group Outreach (DMs)

### Where to look
- Local community Facebook groups ("[City] Neighborhood," "[City] Buy/Sell/Trade")
- Local business groups ("[City] Small Business Network")
- Industry-specific groups (landscaping groups, HVAC groups)
- Look for posts where business owners are introducing themselves or asking for advice

### The tone
Facebook DMs are warmer and more casual than cold calls. Do NOT copy/paste the same message to everyone — people can tell.

**Opening DM:**
> "Hey [Name] — saw your post in [Group Name] / noticed you run [Business Name]. Quick question: are you getting leads from Google right now or are you mostly running on referrals?"

Wait for a reply before saying anything else.

**If they respond and seem open:**
> "That's exactly what we see with a lot of local [industry] businesses — great reputation, but the online side hasn't caught up yet.
>
> We help businesses like yours get more visibility on Google and build up their reviews. Would it be worth hopping on a quick 15-min call to see if it's a fit?"

**If they ask for more info:**
> "Happy to share more — honestly it's pretty quick to explain on a call. We'd look at your current Google presence, see where the gaps are, and tell you exactly what we'd do. If it makes sense, great. If not, no hard feelings."

### DM rules
- **Max 10-15 DMs per day** per account. Facebook flags aggressive DM behavior.
- **Never pitch in the group itself** — always move to DMs.
- **Personalize every opener.** Reference something specific about them.
- **Don't follow up more than twice** if they go quiet. Move on.

## 7. Tracking Your Outreach

Every contact goes into the tracking sheet. No exceptions — if it's not logged, it didn't happen.

**What to log:**
- Business name
- Owner name
- Phone / Facebook profile
- Date contacted
- Channel (in-person / call / DM)
- Outcome: Interested / Not interested / No answer / Callback scheduled / Follow-up needed
- Notes (anything relevant they said)

**Status definitions:**

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| New | Not yet contacted |
| Contacted | Reached out, no response yet |
| Follow-up | They responded, needs another touch |
| Warm | Showed interest, not yet scheduled |
| Call Booked | 15-min call scheduled with team |
| Not a Fit | Rejected or clearly wrong target |

## 8. Handing Off to the Team

When someone books a call or says they want to learn more:

1. Get their **name, number, and best time** to talk
2. Log it in the tracker with status "Call Booked"
3. Send a quick message to Diego with:
   - Business name + owner name
   - Phone number
   - What they said / what they're interested in
   - When they expect a call

Don't promise specifics on pricing, timelines, or deliverables — that's for the team call. Your job is to get them curious and willing to talk. The team handles the rest.

## 9. Common Objections

**"I already have a website."**
> "That's great — most business owners do. The question is whether it's actually bringing in calls. A lot of the sites we look at look fine but don't rank locally. Is yours getting you consistent traffic?"

**"I'm too busy right now."**
> "Totally get it — that's actually a good sign. I'd just hate for you to slow down and then have to scramble for leads. Would it be worth 15 minutes just to see what your options look like? No commitment at all."

**"How much does it cost?"**
> "Honest answer — I don't want to throw a number out without knowing what you actually need. That's what the 15-minute call is for. Our team will look at your specific situation and give you a real answer, not a one-size-fits-all price."

**"I tried marketing before and it didn't work."**
> "I hear that a lot. Can I ask what you tried? ... [listen] ... Yeah, that's actually a common issue with [ads/SEO/etc.] — it's easy to spend money with nothing to show for it. What we do is different — I'd love for our team to show you specifically how. Would you be open to a quick call?"

**"Send me some info."**
> "For sure — I can do that. To make sure I send you the right stuff, can I ask: what's your biggest challenge right now, getting found online or getting people to trust you once they find you?"

(Then follow up with a quick message and a call ask.)

## 11. Daily Goals + Weekly Targets

You need a number to aim for. Without a target, outreach drifts. Here's the baseline:

**Daily targets:**

| Channel | Daily Goal |
|---|---|
| In-person contacts | 5–10 |
| Cold calls (dials) | 30–50 |
| Facebook DMs sent | 10–15 |
| Follow-ups | All due that day |

**Weekly targets:**

| Metric | Weekly Goal |
|---|---|
| Total new contacts | 50–75 |
| Warm leads (showed interest) | 5–10 |
| Calls booked with team | 2–5 |

These are starting targets — we'll adjust as we learn what's working. The point is to show up consistently, not to hit a perfect number every day.

**Track your daily numbers.** At the end of each day, note how many dials, contacts, and DMs you sent. Over time you'll see your own patterns and conversion rates.

## 12. Your First Week

Don't try to do everything at once. Ramp up like this:

**Day 1**
- Read this entire guide
- Set up the tracking spreadsheet
- Join 5-10 local Facebook groups in your target area
- Spend 30 min browsing — get familiar with the groups, who's posting, what businesses are active

**Day 2–3**
- Start with Facebook DMs only — it's the lowest pressure channel
- Send 10-15 DMs per day using the script in Section 6
- Log everything in the tracker
- Get comfortable with responses before adding other channels

**Day 4–5**
- Add in-person outreach — drive around, spot service vehicles, visit Home Depot/Lowe's
- Keep doing DMs daily
- Log everything

**Week 2**
- Add cold calls — start with 20/day and build up to 30-50
- You now have all 3 channels running
- Review what's working and what's not — report back to Diego

**The goal of week 1 is confidence, not volume.** Get comfortable with the conversations before you push the numbers.

## 13. Text Follow-Up Templates

After an in-person meeting or a warm phone call, send a quick text within the hour. Most people forget to do this — it's one of the easiest ways to stand out.

**After an in-person meeting:**
> "Hey [Name], it was great meeting you today! This is [Your Name] from Booked Out. Just wanted to say it was a pleasure chatting — someone from our team will be in touch soon. Feel free to reach out anytime. 😊"

**After a warm call where they want to think about it:**
> "Hey [Name], thanks for taking my call today! This is [Your Name] from Booked Out. No pressure at all — just wanted to send a quick note. If you ever have questions or want to chat, I'm easy to reach. Have a great day!"

**Follow-up if they go quiet after showing interest:**
> "Hey [Name]! Following up from our conversation earlier this week. Just wanted to check in — still happy to set up that quick call whenever works for you. No rush at all."

**Rules for texts:**
- Keep them short and warm — not salesy
- Send within 1 hour of the conversation while it's fresh
- Max 2 follow-up texts if they go quiet — then move on
- Never text after 7pm

## 14. What NOT to Do

These are the mistakes that kill deals before they start:

**Don't lead with price.** Ever. Not even a range. The moment you say a number before they're interested, you lose them. Price comes on the team call.

**Don't oversell.** If you catch yourself using phrases like "this will completely transform your business" — stop. Be real. Overpromising kills trust.

**Don't pitch in Facebook groups.** Post in a group even once and you'll get banned and flagged. Always move to DMs first.

**Don't lie or make things up.** If you don't know the answer, say "great question — our team will cover that on the call." Never invent details about pricing, timelines, or what's included.

**Don't chase hard no's.** If someone says no clearly and firmly, move on. Pushing past a hard no damages your reputation and wastes time.

**Don't forget to log.** Every single contact. If it's not in the tracker, for us it didn't happen.

**Don't make it weird.** You're a real person starting a real conversation. If you act like a robot reading from a script, people feel it. Be yourself.

**Don't skip the follow-up text.** It takes 30 seconds and it makes a real difference.

## 15. How to Find Facebook Groups

**Step 1: Search for local groups**
In Facebook's search bar, search for:
- "[Your City] neighborhood"
- "[Your City] community"
- "[Your City] small business"
- "[Your City] buy sell trade"
- "[Your City] home services"
- "[Your City] landscaping" / "[Your City] HVAC" etc.

**Step 2: Join the right ones**
Look for groups with:
- 500+ members (active, not dead)
- Posts in the last 7 days
- Business owners posting and engaging (not just residents)

Join 10-15 groups. You don't need to be in 100 — you need to be active in a few good ones.

**Step 3: Lurk before you DM**
Spend 2-3 days just watching. See who's posting, who's a business owner, what problems people are asking about. Then start DMs from there — you'll have more context and your openers will feel natural.

**Step 4: Find prospects inside groups**
Look for:
- Posts where someone introduces their business
- Posts asking for recommendations ("who do you use for lawn care?") — the businesses that reply are your prospects
- New member posts from business owners
- Posts where someone mentions struggling with leads or business being slow

**Step 5: Keep a short list**
Before you start DMing each day, identify 10-15 specific people to message. Don't just browse and DM randomly — it leads to lazy, generic messages.

## 16. End of Day Routine (10 minutes)

Before you wrap up each day:

1. **Update the tracker** — make sure every contact from today is logged with current status
2. **Set follow-up dates** — anyone who needs a callback or follow-up, set the date in the tracker now
3. **Send Diego a quick summary** — just a few lines:
   - How many contacts today (in-person / calls / DMs)
   - Any warm leads or booked calls
   - Anything notable (great conversation, common objection you heard, etc.)
4. **Prep tomorrow's list** — identify who you're going to reach out to tomorrow so you're not starting from scratch in the morning

This takes 10 minutes max and keeps everything clean. It also keeps Diego in the loop without needing to micromanage.

**Example daily summary to Diego:**
> "Today: 8 in-person, 35 calls, 12 DMs. 2 warm leads — Mario from Green Lawn (said to call back Thursday) and Susan from Crystal Clean (wants more info, will text tomorrow). One common objection today: people keep saying they already have a website. Thoughts?"
